Academic Interests

My primary academic interests lie in both pure and applied mathematics. On the pure side, I am fascinated by algebra, number theory, and the deep structures that underpin mathematical systems. On the applied side, I explore dynamical systems, nonlinear analysis, economics, and cryptography, seeking to understand real-world phenomena through rigorous quantitative frameworks.

I enjoy combining theoretical understanding with practical problem-solving, often bridging abstract mathematics with computational models, simulations, and experimental applications.
